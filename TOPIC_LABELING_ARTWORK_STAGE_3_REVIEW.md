## 2. Decision Label Legend

### Approved

Approved for public topic copy. Use as orientation for senior supply chain leaders.

### Revise

Useful but wording is too strong, too legalistic, too detailed, too operationally specific, or needs senior-leader orientation language. Reviewer note should carry the proposed reframe.

### Keep internal

Useful for editorial judgment, QA stress-testing, risk awareness, implementation planning, or client engagement diligence. Do not appear publicly unless later converted into an approved public-safe Common Pitfall or what-to-ask-next prompt.

### Source gap

May be correct, but source, citation, current enforcement posture, exact wording, or current revision requires verification before publication.

### Exclude

Out of scope, not useful to the target user, belongs in another topic, or outside the current US/EU scope.

## 3. Stage 3 Decision Rubric

Stage 3 is not a legal interpretation exercise from scratch. It is a publication-readiness review of Brian / ISC input notes.

For each item, Brian / ISC should decide whether the note is safe, useful, source-supported, and appropriately worded for public topic content.

**Six review questions:**

1. **Is it source-backed?** If yes, it may be approved or revised. If no or uncertain, mark `Source gap`.
2. **Is it useful to a senior supply chain leader?** If yes, keep considering. If no, mark `Exclude` or `Keep internal`.
3. **Is it phrased as orientation rather than legal determination?** If yes, it may be public. If no, mark `Revise`.
4. **Does it avoid overclaiming?** If yes, it may be public. If it uses strong legal language such as "must," "shall," "violation," "liable," "adulterated," "misbranded," "direct violation," "critical violation," or "fast track to a 483," mark `Revise` or `Keep internal`.
5. **Does it fit the Labeling & Artwork topic?** If yes, keep. If it belongs more naturally in Packaging, Serialization, Distribution & 3PLs, Returns, Recalls, or Release & Disposition, cross-link, move, or exclude.
6. **Is it current enough?** If current enforcement posture matters, mark `Source gap` unless Brian / ISC has verified currency.
